About Me

Hi, I'm Desmond. But my friends call me Des.

I'm of the firm belief that digital independence is a fundamental human right. We live so much of our lives online now that being able to navigate that space on your own terms isn't just a convenience—it's a necessity. My favorite thing to do is help my friends unlock that independence for themselves.

The tools you need to succeed are already out there. Sometimes, you just need a hand finding the ones that fit your life. I love helping people explore those options and sticking with them until they're ready to conquer the digital domain solo.

The things I know best

I've spent years diving deep into the tech that makes the web accessible. If you're curious about any of these, I'm your guy:

Screen Readers: Getting the most out of JAWS, NVDA, or VoiceOver.

Braille: Making refreshable braille displays feel like second nature.

Magnification: Tweaking settings for low-vision users so everything is clear.

Voice Control: Navigating and typing using only your voice.

Keyboard Navigation: Learning the shortcuts to move lightning-fast and leave your mouse-using friends in the dust.

Why this matters to me

The internet meant everything to me when I was a teenager. It was the place where I actually met my friends, formed real bonds, discovered my favorite music, and—naturally—watched way too many viral videos (yes, including Llamas with Hats).

But more than just entertainment, the internet was my classroom. It was where I went to learn... well, everything. It wasn't all "good" stuff, but it was my source of information.

Today, I'm dedicated to making sure everyone can tap into that same resource. Information is out there, and regardless of how you interact with your technology, I want to show you how to get to it. Whether it's the absolute basics of using a computer or learning to fly through websites with a screen reader, I just want to help you claim your independence.

If you're interested in working with me or just want to send me a message, check out my Contact Page. Or say hello on LinkedIn!

Thanks for reading,
--Des
